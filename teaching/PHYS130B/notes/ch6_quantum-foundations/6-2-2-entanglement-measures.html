6.2.2 Entanglement Measures#

Prompts

  • What is entanglement entropy? How do you compute it for a bipartite pure state from the reduced density matrix?

  • For a Bell state (maximally entangled), what is the entanglement entropy? What does maximum entanglement look like?

  • What are Rényi entropies, and why is Rényi-2 easier to measure experimentally than full von Neumann entropy?

  • How does mutual information relate to entanglement entropy? Can two unentangled states have mutual information?

  • What does an entanglement witness measure? How do you construct one, and what can you conclude from it?

Lecture Notes#

Entanglement is not merely a binary property (entangled or not)—it admits a rich quantitative structure. How entangled is a given state? Can we assign a number to it? The answers draw on information theory, geometry, and operator theory, and have deep implications for quantum computing, communication, and the structure of many-body physics.

Entanglement Entropy#

Discussion

Entanglement entropy and entanglement of formation agree for pure states but not mixed states. Construct a 2-qubit mixed state where E_F ≠ S(ρ_A) and compute both.

Definition: Entanglement Entropy

For a bipartite pure state \(|\Psi\rangle_{AB}\) with Schmidt decomposition \(|\Psi\rangle = \sum_k \lambda_k |u_k\rangle_A |v_k\rangle_B\), the entanglement entropy is defined as the von Neumann entropy of the reduced density matrix:

(241)#\[ E(\Psi) = S(\rho_A) = -\text{Tr}(\rho_A \ln \rho_A) = -\sum_k \lambda_k^2 \ln \lambda_k^2\]

where \(\rho_A = \text{Tr}_B(|\Psi\rangle\langle\Psi|)\) has eigenvalues \(\lambda_k^2\) (the squared Schmidt coefficients from §6.2.1).

Key properties:

  • Vanishes for product states. If \(|\Psi\rangle = |\psi\rangle_A \otimes |\phi\rangle_B\), then \(\rho_A\) is pure and \(E = 0\).

  • Maximized for maximally entangled states. For two qubits, the Bell states achieve \(E = \ln 2\) (1 ebit).

  • Symmetric. \(S(\rho_A) = S(\rho_B)\) for any bipartite pure state—a remarkable consequence of the Schmidt decomposition.

  • Monotone. Cannot increase under local operations and classical communication (LOCC).

Example: Bell State Entanglement Entropy

For the Bell state \(|\Phi^+\rangle = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|00\rangle + |11\rangle)\), the reduced density matrix is:

\[ \rho_A = \frac{1}{2}|0\rangle\langle 0| + \frac{1}{2}|1\rangle\langle 1| = \frac{I}{2}\]

Eigenvalues: \(\lambda_1^2 = \lambda_2^2 = 1/2\). The entanglement entropy is:

\[ E = -\frac{1}{2}\ln\frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2}\ln\frac{1}{2} = \ln 2 \approx 0.693 \text{ nats}\]

This is maximal for two qubits: one ebit of entanglement.

Example: Partially Entangled State

Consider \(|\Psi\rangle = \cos\theta\, |00\rangle + \sin\theta\, |11\rangle\) with \(0 < \theta < \pi/4\).

Schmidt coefficients: \(\lambda_1 = \cos\theta\), \(\lambda_2 = \sin\theta\). The entanglement entropy is:

\[ E(\theta) = -\cos^2\theta\, \ln(\cos^2\theta) - \sin^2\theta\, \ln(\sin^2\theta)\]

  • At \(\theta = 0\): product state, \(E = 0\).

  • At \(\theta = \pi/4\): Bell state, \(E = \ln 2\) (maximum).

  • For intermediate \(\theta\): \(0 < E < \ln 2\) (partial entanglement).

The entanglement entropy interpolates smoothly between separable and maximally entangled.

Rényi Entanglement Entropies#

The Rényi-\(\alpha\) entanglement entropy generalizes entanglement entropy:

(242)#\[ E_\alpha(\Psi) = S_\alpha(\rho_A) = \frac{1}{1-\alpha} \ln \text{Tr}(\rho_A^\alpha) = \frac{1}{1-\alpha} \ln \sum_k \lambda_k^{2\alpha}\]

  • \(\alpha \to 1\): recovers von Neumann entanglement entropy.

  • \(\alpha = 2\): Rényi-2 entropy \(E_2 = -\ln \text{Tr}(\rho_A^2) = -\ln(\text{purity})\), experimentally accessible via randomized measurements without full tomography.

  • \(\alpha = 0\): \(E_0 = \ln(\text{Schmidt rank})\), counts the dimension of entanglement.

Note

Rényi-2 entropy is particularly important in experiments because \(\text{Tr}(\rho_A^2)\) can be measured by performing identical measurements on two copies of the state (the “swap trick”), avoiding the need for full quantum state tomography.

Concurrence (Two Qubits)#

For two-qubit systems, a computable entanglement measure is the concurrence \(\mathcal{C}\).

#:::{admonition} Definition: Concurrence (Pure Two-Qubit States) :class: important

For a pure two-qubit state \(|\Psi\rangle = \sum_{ij} c_{ij}|ij\rangle\):

(243)#\[ \mathcal{C}(|\Psi\rangle) = 2|\det(C)| = 2|c_{00}c_{11} - c_{01}c_{10}|\]

where \(C = [c_{ij}]\) is the \(2\times 2\) coefficient matrix.

  • \(\mathcal{C} = 0\): separable (product state).

  • \(\mathcal{C} = 1\): maximally entangled (Bell state).

Example: Concurrence of a General Two-Qubit State

For \(|\Psi\rangle = \alpha|00\rangle + \beta|11\rangle\) (with \(|\alpha|^2 + |\beta|^2 = 1\)):

\[\begin{split} C = \begin{pmatrix} \alpha & 0 \\ 0 & \beta \end{pmatrix}, \quad \mathcal{C} = 2|\alpha\beta|\end{split}\]

  • \(\alpha = 1, \beta = 0\): \(\mathcal{C} = 0\) (product state \(|00\rangle\)).

  • \(\alpha = \beta = 1/\sqrt{2}\): \(\mathcal{C} = 1\) (Bell state \(|\Phi^+\rangle\)).

  • \(\alpha = \sqrt{3}/2, \beta = 1/2\): \(\mathcal{C} = \sqrt{3}/2 \approx 0.87\) (partially entangled).

Mixed States (Wootters’ Formula)#

For mixed two-qubit states, Wootters’ concurrence is one of the few computable entanglement measures. It requires computing a “spin-flipped” version of the density matrix:

(244)#\[ \tilde{\rho} = (\hat{\sigma}^y \otimes \hat{\sigma}^y) \rho^* (\hat{\sigma}^y \otimes \hat{\sigma}^y)\]

where \(\rho^*\) is complex conjugation in the computational basis, and \(\hat{\sigma}^y\) are Pauli matrices.

Wootters’ Concurrence for Mixed States

Compute the eigenvalues \(\mu_1 \geq \mu_2 \geq \mu_3 \geq \mu_4\) of the matrix product \(\sqrt{\sqrt{\rho} \tilde{\rho} \sqrt{\rho}}\), and evaluate:

(245)#\[ \mathcal{C}(\rho) = \max(0, \mu_1 - \mu_2 - \mu_3 - \mu_4)\]

Properties:

  • \(\mathcal{C}(\rho) \in [0, 1]\) for two-qubit systems

  • \(\mathcal{C} = 0\) iff the state is separable

  • \(\mathcal{C} = 1\) iff the state is maximally entangled (Bell state)

  • This formula is one of the few computable entanglement measures for mixed states.

Derivation: Wootters’ Concurrence Formula

Construction of the formula:

The concurrence for a pure state was: \(\mathcal{C}(|\Psi\rangle) = 2|c_1 c_2|\) (product of Schmidt coefficients scaled).

For mixed states, Wootters generalized this by introducing an auxiliary “spin-flipped” version:

\[ \tilde{\rho} = (\hat{\sigma}^y \otimes \hat{\sigma}^y) \rho^* (\hat{\sigma}^y \otimes \hat{\sigma}^y)\]

The idea: \(\tilde{\rho}\) transforms the state in a specific way related to its entanglement structure.

Key steps:

  1. Compute \(R = \sqrt{\sqrt{\rho} \tilde{\rho} \sqrt{\rho}}\) (the geometric mean of \(\rho\) and \(\tilde{\rho}\)).

  2. Find eigenvalues \(\mu_j\) of \(R\) in decreasing order.

  3. Apply the formula: \(\mathcal{C}(\rho) = \max(0, \mu_1 - \mu_2 - \mu_3 - \mu_4)\).

The max with 0 ensures concurrence is non-negative; separable states automatically have \(\mathcal{C} = 0\).

Relation to Entanglement of Formation#

The entanglement of formation \(E_f\) (minimum average entanglement over all pure-state decompositions of \(\rho\)) is related to concurrence by:

(246)#\[ E_f(\rho) = h\!\left(\frac{1 + \sqrt{1 - \mathcal{C}^2}}{2}\right)\]

where \(h(x) = -x\ln x - (1-x)\ln(1-x)\) is the binary entropy function.

Entanglement Witnesses#

An entanglement witness is a Hermitian operator \(W\) that detects entanglement without full state tomography.

#:::{admonition} Definition: Entanglement Witness :class: important

\(W\) is an entanglement witness if:

  • \(\text{Tr}(W \rho_{\text{sep}}) \geq 0\) for all separable states \(\rho_{\text{sep}}\).

  • \(\text{Tr}(W \rho_{\text{ent}}) < 0\) for at least one entangled state \(\rho_{\text{ent}}\).

A negative expectation value \(\langle W \rangle < 0\) certifies that the state is entangled.

A systematic construction of entanglement witnesses:

Construction: Entanglement Witnesses

For the Bell state \(|\Phi^+\rangle\), a witness is:

(247)#\[ W = \frac{1}{2}I - |\Phi^+\rangle\langle\Phi^+|\]

  • For any separable state: \(\langle\Phi^+|\rho_{\text{sep}}|\Phi^+\rangle \leq 1/2\), so \(\text{Tr}(W\rho_{\text{sep}}) \geq 0\).

  • For \(\rho = |\Phi^+\rangle\langle\Phi^+|\): \(\text{Tr}(W\rho) = 1/2 - 1 = -1/2 < 0\).

Example: Detecting Entanglement with a Witness

Consider the noisy Bell state \(\rho = p |\Phi^+\rangle\langle\Phi^+| + (1-p)\frac{I}{4}\) (Werner state).

Using the witness \(W = \frac{1}{2}I - |\Phi^+\rangle\langle\Phi^+|\):

\[ \text{Tr}(W\rho) = \frac{1}{2} - p - (1-p)\frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{2} - p - \frac{1}{4} + \frac{p}{4} = \frac{1}{4} - \frac{3p}{4}\]

This is negative when \(p > 1/3\). So the witness detects entanglement for \(p > 1/3\), even though the state is mixed with significant noise.

Important

By the Hahn–Banach theorem, every entangled state can be detected by some witness. However, no single witness detects all entangled states—different states require different witnesses. Finding optimal witnesses is an active area of research.

Definition: Negativity

The negativity exploits the partial transpose criterion (Peres–Horodecki):

(248)#\[ \mathcal{N}(\rho) = \frac{\|\rho^{T_B}\|_1 - 1}{2}\]

where \(\rho^{T_B}\) is the partial transpose with respect to subsystem B, and \(\|\cdot\|_1\) is the trace norm (\(\sum_i |\lambda_i|\)).

  • \(\mathcal{N} = 0\): state has positive partial transpose (PPT); separable for \(2 \times 2\) and \(2 \times 3\) systems.

  • \(\mathcal{N} > 0\): state is entangled (negative eigenvalue under partial transpose).

  • Logarithmic negativity \(E_N = \ln\|\rho^{T_B}\|_1\) is also widely used.

Caution

In dimensions \(\geq 3 \times 3\), there exist entangled states with positive partial transpose (PPT entangled states, or “bound entangled” states). Negativity fails to detect these; additional criteria are needed.

Note

All valid entanglement measures must satisfy: (1) vanish on separable states, (2) not increase under LOCC (monotonicity), and (3) reduce to entanglement entropy on pure states. These axioms define an entanglement monotone.

Summary#

  • Entanglement entropy \(E = S(\rho_A)\): fundamental measure for pure bipartite states; ranges from 0 (separable) to \(\ln d\) (maximally entangled).

  • Rényi entropies \(E_\alpha\): generalize entanglement entropy; \(E_2\) is experimentally accessible via the swap trick.

  • Concurrence \(\mathcal{C}\): computable for two-qubit states (pure and mixed); ranges from 0 to 1.

  • Entanglement witnesses: operators detecting entanglement via \(\langle W \rangle < 0\); every entangled state has a witness (Hahn–Banach).

  • Negativity: uses partial transpose; computable but misses bound entangled states in high dimensions.

  • No single measure suffices: different measures capture distinct operational aspects (distillation, formation, detection).

Homework#

1. Schmidt Decomposition and Entanglement Rank. Any bipartite pure state can be written as \(|\psi\rangle = \sum_i \sqrt{\lambda_i}\, |a_i\rangle|b_i\rangle\) with orthonormal bases on each side. Find the Schmidt decomposition of:

\[ |\psi\rangle = \frac{1}{2}(|00\rangle + |01\rangle + |10\rangle - |11\rangle)\]

What is the Schmidt rank? What does this tell you about how entangled the state is?

2. Product States Are Unentangled. Show that if \(|\psi\rangle = |\phi\rangle_A \otimes |\phi\rangle_B\) is a product state, then: (a) The reduced density matrix \(\hat{\rho}_A = \mathrm{Tr}_B(|\psi\rangle\langle\psi|)\) is pure. (b) The entanglement entropy is exactly zero: \(E(\psi) = 0\). (c) The Schmidt rank is exactly 1.

What does the Schmidt rank \(\geq 2\) imply about a state?

3. Bell States Are Maximally Entangled. For all four Bell states:

\[ |\Phi^\pm\rangle = \frac{|00\rangle \pm |11\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}, \quad |\Psi^\pm\rangle = \frac{|01\rangle \pm |10\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}\]

(a) Compute the reduced density matrix \(\hat{\rho}_A = \frac{I}{2}\) for each. (b) Show the entanglement entropy is \(E = \ln 2\) for all four. (c) Verify this is the maximum possible for two qubits: \(\max(E) = \ln d_A = \ln 2\).

4. Partial Entanglement Interpolates. Consider \(|\psi(\theta)\rangle = \cos\theta\, |00\rangle + \sin\theta\, |11\rangle\) for \(\theta \in [0, \pi/2]\).

(a) Find the Schmidt coefficients \(\lambda_1(\theta)\) and \(\lambda_2(\theta)\). (b) Compute the entanglement entropy \(E(\theta) = -\sum_i \lambda_i^2 \ln(\lambda_i^2)\). (c) Show that \(E(0) = 0\) (product state) and \(E(\pi/4) = \ln 2\) (Bell state). (d) Sketch \(E(\theta)\) and explain its physical meaning.

5. Rényi-2 Entropy and Purity. The Rényi-2 entanglement entropy is \(E_2 = -\ln\mathrm{Tr}(\hat{\rho}_A^2)\).

(a) For a pure subsystem, show that \(\mathrm{Tr}(\hat{\rho}_A^2) = 1\) and thus \(E_2 = 0\). (b) For the Bell state reduced density matrix \(\hat{\rho}_A = \frac{I}{2}\), compute \(E_2\) and compare to the von Neumann entropy \(E_1\). (c) Why is \(E_2\) experimentally useful? (Hint: think about the “swap trick”.)

6. Concurrence for Two-Qubit Pure States. For a pure two-qubit state \(|\Psi\rangle = \alpha|00\rangle + \beta|01\rangle + \gamma|10\rangle + \delta|11\rangle\), the concurrence is:

\[ \mathcal{C}(|\Psi\rangle) = 2|\alpha\delta - \beta\gamma|\]

(a) Verify that \(\mathcal{C} = 1\) for the Bell state \(|\Phi^+\rangle = \frac{|00\rangle + |11\rangle}{\sqrt{2}}\). (b) Verify that \(\mathcal{C} = 0\) for product state \(|\psi\rangle = |0\rangle_A \otimes |+\rangle_B\). (c) For the partially entangled state \(|\psi(\theta)\rangle = \cos\theta\, |00\rangle + \sin\theta\, |11\rangle\), show that \(\mathcal{C}(\theta) = \sin(2\theta)\).

7. Entanglement of Formation vs. Concurrence. Wootters showed that for two qubits:

\[ E_f(\rho) = h\left(\frac{1 + \sqrt{1 - \mathcal{C}^2}}{2}\right)\]

where \(h(x) = -x\ln x - (1-x)\ln(1-x)\) is the binary entropy.

(a) For the Bell state (\(\mathcal{C} = 1\)), compute \(E_f\) and verify it equals \(\ln 2\). (b) For \(\mathcal{C} = 1/2\), compute \(E_f\) numerically. (c) Explain why \(E_f(\rho) \geq E_d(\rho)\) (entanglement of formation is at least distillable entanglement).

8. Entanglement Witnesses Detect Entanglement. For the Bell state \(|\Phi^+\rangle\), define the witness:

\[ W = \frac{I}{2} - |\Phi^+\rangle\langle\Phi^+|\]

(a) Show that for any separable state \(\rho_{\mathrm{sep}}\), we have \(\mathrm{Tr}(W\rho_{\mathrm{sep}}) \geq 0\). (b) Compute \(\mathrm{Tr}(W|\Phi^+\rangle\langle\Phi^+|)\) and verify it is negative. (c) For the Werner state \(\rho = p|\Phi^+\rangle\langle\Phi^+| + (1-p)\frac{I}{4}\), find the threshold \(p_c\) above which the witness detects entanglement. (d) Why is no single witness able to detect all entangled states?

9. Negativity as an Entanglement Measure. The partial transpose (with respect to subsystem B) of a \(2 \times 2\) state is \(\rho^{T_B}\). The negativity is:

\[ \mathcal{N}(\rho) = \frac{\|\rho^{T_B}\|_1 - 1}{2}\]

where \(\|\cdot\|_1\) is the trace norm (sum of absolute values of eigenvalues).

(a) For the Bell state, compute \(\rho^{T_B}\) and show that \(\mathcal{N} > 0\) (has negative eigenvalues). (b) For a product state \(\rho = \rho_A \otimes \rho_B\), show that \(\rho^{T_B}\) is also separable, so \(\mathcal{N} = 0\). (c) Why does the partial transpose criterion fail for bound entangled states in dimensions \(\geq 3 \times 3\)?
