2.3.2 Fractional Quantum Hall Anyons#

Prompts

  • What is the fractional quantum Hall effect, and at what filling fractions does it occur?

  • What are the charge and statistics of the \(e/3\) anyon at \(\nu = 1/3\)?

  • How does the charge-flux composite picture explain why quasiparticles have fractional statistics?

Lecture Notes#

The Fractional Quantum Hall Effect#

When a 2D electron gas is placed in a strong perpendicular magnetic field at low temperature, the Hall conductance is quantized. At certain fractional filling factors, the system forms an incompressible quantum liquid:

Key Filling Fractions

The fractional quantum Hall effect (FQHE) occurs at filling fractions \(\nu = 1/3, 2/5, 1/5, 2/3, \ldots\) The most prominent is \(\nu = 1/3\), discovered by Tsui, Störmer, and Gossard in 1982 (Nobel Prize 1998).

The FQHE cannot be explained by single-particle physics — it arises from strong electron-electron correlations. The ground state is described by the Laughlin wavefunction:

\[\psi(z_1, \ldots, z_N) = \prod_{i < j} (z_i - z_j)^m \, \mathrm{e}^{-\sum_k |z_k|^2 / 4\ell_B^2}\]

where \(z_k = x_k + \mathrm{i} y_k\) are complex coordinates, \(m\) is an odd integer, \(\ell_B = \sqrt{\hbar/eB}\) is the magnetic length, and \(\nu = 1/m\).

Quasiparticle Properties at \(\nu = 1/3\)

The elementary excitations (quasiparticles) of the \(\nu = 1/3\) state are anyons with:

  • Fractional charge: \(q = e/3\) (one-third of the electron charge)

  • Fractional statistics: statistical angle \(\theta = \pi/3\) (exchange phase \(\mathrm{e}^{\mathrm{i}\pi/3}\))

These properties have been confirmed experimentally through shot noise measurements (charge) and interferometry (statistics).

The Charge-Flux Composite Picture#

The origin of fractional statistics can be understood through a simple and powerful picture: each anyon is a charge bound to a magnetic flux tube.

Charge-Flux Composite

Attach a fictitious flux tube \(\Phi\) to each particle of charge \(q\). When one composite moves around another, the charge picks up an Aharonov-Bohm phase:

\[\theta_{\text{AB}} = \frac{q \Phi}{\hbar}\]

For the exchange (half-loop), the statistical angle is:

\[\theta = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{q \Phi}{\hbar}\]

Example: \(\nu = 1/3\) Anyons

At \(\nu = 1/3\), each electron binds 3 magnetic flux quanta \(\Phi_0 = h/e\). A quasihole carries charge \(q = e/3\) and flux \(\Phi = \Phi_0/3\). The braiding phase for taking one quasihole around another is:

\[\theta_{\text{braid}} = \frac{(e/3)(\Phi_0/3)}{\hbar} = \frac{(e/3)(h/3e)}{\hbar} = \frac{2\pi}{3}\]

Since exchange is half a braid: \(\theta_{\text{exchange}} = \pi/3\). This matches the known statistical angle.

This picture is powerful because it reduces the exotic phenomenon of fractional statistics to the familiar Aharonov-Bohm effect: a charge moving around a flux picks up a phase proportional to the enclosed flux.

Summary

The FQHE demonstrates that:

  1. Fractionalization is real: electrons in 2D can collectively produce excitations with fractional charge

  2. Statistics is emergent: the anyonic statistics of quasiparticles is not put in by hand — it emerges from the many-body ground state

  3. Topology protects: the fractional charge and statistics are topological invariants, robust against local perturbations

Discussion: Is \(e/3\) a Real Charge?

A quasihole in the \(\nu = 1/3\) state carries charge \(e/3\), but it is not literally one-third of an electron — it is a collective excitation of many electrons. In what sense is the fractional charge “real”? Can you measure \(e/3\) in a single experiment, or only statistically? What would Millikan’s oil drop experiment show if you could trap a single quasihole?

Homework#

1. State the filling fraction, quasiparticle charge, and statistical angle for the \(\nu = 1/3\) fractional quantum Hall state.

2. Using the charge-flux composite picture: if a particle of charge \(q\) is bound to a flux tube \(\Phi\), and another identical composite orbits around it in a full loop, what is the Aharonov-Bohm phase? What is the exchange phase (half-loop)?

3. At filling \(\nu = 1/5\), the quasihole has charge \(e/5\). Using the charge-flux picture, compute the statistical angle \(\theta\) for exchanging two such quasiholes.

4. The Laughlin wavefunction at \(\nu = 1/m\) contains the factor \(\prod_{i<j}(z_i - z_j)^m\). Explain in words why this factor vanishes when two electrons approach each other, and what this has to do with the Pauli principle (for odd \(m\)).

5. True or false (explain briefly): (a) Anyons with \(\theta = \pi/3\) are bosons. (b) The fractional charge \(e/3\) has been measured experimentally. (c) The charge-flux picture works because of the Aharonov-Bohm effect. (d) Fractional statistics can occur in 3D bulk materials.

6. If you braid one \(\nu = 1/3\) quasihole around another three times (three full loops), what is the total accumulated phase? Express your answer as a multiple of \(\pi\).
